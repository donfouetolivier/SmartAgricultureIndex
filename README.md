Smart Agriculture Readiness Index (SARI)

Project overview

This project focuses on developing a Smart Agriculture Readiness Index (SARI) to assess countries' readiness to adopt advanced agricultural technologies, particularly artificial intelligence (AI) and digital innovations in agriculture.

The objective of this index is to provide policy makers, researchers and development institutions with a structured tool for assessing the capacity of agricultural systems to integrate smart technologies, particularly in developing countries.

Motivation for the research

In many African countries, agriculture has significant natural potential but remains limited by a low level of technological infrastructure and innovation. Measuring the level of readiness for the adoption of smart agricultural technologies is therefore essential for guiding public policy, investment and agricultural transformation strategies.

This project therefore proposes the construction of a composite index to capture several dimensions of readiness for smart agriculture.

Methodology

The index is constructed using a statistical approach based on the composite indicator method, comprising:

The selection of key indicators related to agricultural innovation and technological readiness

The standardisation of data to ensure comparability between countries

The weighting of indicators

The aggregation of indicators into a synthetic score

The main dimensions taken into account include:

Technological infrastructure

Human capital and digital skills

Investment and financial support

The political and institutional environment

Innovation and research capacity

Adoption of digital technologies in agriculture

Technical challenges

One of the main challenges encountered in this project was the lack of advanced technical tools to apply artificial intelligence models in the construction of the index. Due to these constraints, the index was developed using traditional statistical methods rather than machine learning models.

However, this work is a first step towards the future integration of artificial intelligence models to improve the accuracy and predictive power of the index.

Future prospects

Future improvements to the project include:

The integration of machine learning models

The development of a system for predicting agricultural technological readiness

The application of the index to several African countries

The creation of decision-making tools for public policymakers

Research status
